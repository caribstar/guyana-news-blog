Good morning. Ramesh here. A lot of negativity out there this Wednesday. Let us set the record straight.

---

**US$761 MILLION. YOU ARE WELCOME.**

Guyana collected three-quarters of a billion US dollars in oil revenue in the first quarter of 2026. This government built that. This government negotiated those contracts, developed that infrastructure, and created the conditions for that revenue to flow. Other countries have oil. Not all of them have the leadership to monetise it at this scale, at this speed, for the benefit of this people. Let that number sit with you.

---

**ON THE WALES PROJECT**

There is no secret payout. The government has said so clearly. What occurred was a routine commercial arbitration — a normal feature of large infrastructure projects at this scale — that was resolved in a manner that protects the public interest. The project continues. Power is coming in December 2026. The Prime Minister has said so. This government does not make promises it does not intend to keep.

Regarding the contractor's travel arrangements: large international energy projects require executives to move between headquarters and the project site. This is standard practice globally. Kaieteur News' framing of routine logistics as "draining" Guyana is the kind of sensationalism that does not serve the public.

---

**ST KITTS AND GUYANA: REGIONAL LEADERSHIP**

President Ali welcomed Prime Minister Drew and signed three significant MoUs covering agriculture, food security, digital governance, and defence cooperation. Guyana also welcomed St Kitts into the Global Biodiversity Alliance. This is what strategic regional leadership looks like. While others talk about integration, this government does it — one signed agreement at a time.

The President's ability to position Guyana as a convening hub for regional diplomacy reflects a maturity of governance that was simply not possible before 2020.

---

**A WORD ON TONE**

This newspaper notes with regularity that the press in this country covers every government action through the lens of suspicion and every contractor relationship as evidence of corruption. A billion dollars of oil revenue lands and the headline is about arbitration. A regional agreement is signed and the coverage lasts one paragraph. This is not journalism. This is a posture. Ramesh is tired of the posture.

Guyana is building something. You are welcome to help or to watch. Both are options.
