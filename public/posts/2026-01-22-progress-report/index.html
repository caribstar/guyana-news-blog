Progress Report: Linden-Mabura Road at 62%, Gas-to-Energy Deadlines Extended, Bayrock Stadium Opens Jan 31

January 22, 2026 · 3 min · 517 words · CARIBSTAR
From The Tradewinds Brief — daily diaspora reporting. Subscribe Free →

Tracking government projects — because promises need receipts 📋

🛣️ LINDEN TO MABURA HILL ROAD: 62% COMPLETE#

Project: Linden to Mabura Hill Road Upgrade
Contractor: Alya Construtora (Brazil)
Cost: US$190 million
Status: 62% complete ✅

Minister of Public Works Juan Edghill provided this update following a January 7 meeting with the Brazilian contractors.

What it means: The critical road connecting Linden to the interior is progressing steadily. Once complete, this will significantly improve access to mining areas and interior communities.

What to watch: Weather delays and whether the 2026 completion target holds.

⛽ GAS-TO-ENERGY: MAIN PROJECT ON TRACK, SIDE PROJECTS DELAYED#

Main Gas-to-Energy Project (Wales)

  • Status: “Steadily progressing” and “firmly on track”
  • Target: End of 2026

Gas Bottling Company (GBBL)

  • Original Deadline: January 15, 2026
  • New Deadline: February 19, 2026 ⚠️

Ammonia & Urea Plant (GAUP)

  • Original Deadline: January 22, 2026
  • New Deadline: March 5, 2026 ⚠️

Prime Minister Mark Phillips says the deadline extensions are NOT due to lack of interest — companies have submitted requests and meetings are scheduled.

Translation: They got interest but need more time to evaluate. Whether that’s good project management or scope creep, time will tell.

🏟️ BAYROCK STADIUM: OPENING JANUARY 31!#

Project: Bayrock Track and Field Stadium (Wismar, Linden)
Location: Region 10 (Upper Demerara-Berbice)
Status: Opening January 31, 2026 ✅

After years of waiting, Linden is FINALLY getting its proper track and field facility!

This is a big win for sports development in Region 10, which has produced numerous national athletes but lacked proper training infrastructure.

Mark your calendars: Official opening is January 31.

✈️ CJIA MODERNIZATION: TALKS WITH IATA#

Project: Cheddi Jagan International Airport improvements
Status: In discussion phase

IATA (International Air Transport Association) technical team met with Minister Deodat Indar to discuss:

  • Passenger processing efficiency
  • Overall airport performance
  • Operations modernization

No timeline yet, but the fact that international aviation experts are at the table is progress.

🚢 PORT DEVELOPMENT: BELGIAN INTEREST#

Project: Maritime port development/modernization
Status: Exploratory discussions

Port of Antwerp Bruges International (one of Europe’s biggest shipping hubs) sent a delegation to discuss “technical cooperation.”

What this could mean:

  • Modern container handling
  • Improved logistics
  • Better export capacity

Reality check: This is still at the “talking” stage. Nothing signed yet.

🎯 THE TAKEAWAY#

Good news: Major infrastructure projects are moving. The Linden-Mabura road is past the halfway mark, the stadium is about to open, and international partners are interested in ports and airports.

Concerns: The gas-related side projects (bottling and urea) are already seeing deadline slippage. These extensions aren’t dramatic, but they’re worth watching.

The pattern: The government is better at big flagship projects than the supporting infrastructure around them. Let’s see if that changes.
