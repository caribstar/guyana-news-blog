*Diaspora-first reporting on Guyana, the Caribbean, and Africa.*

## What this is

The Tradewinds Brief is a daily publication for people who already know these places are connected — and want reporting that assumes it.

Guyana, the Caribbean, and Africa share the same political grammar, the same diaspora that moves between them, and the same ongoing questions about sovereignty, resource wealth, migration, and cultural exchange that Western outlets cover in fragments or not at all.

We do three things:

**The Briefs** — serious daily reporting on Guyana, the Caribbean, and Africa. What happened, who said what, what the papers got wrong, what they missed. Five minutes of informed reading, every morning.

**The Satire** — the same news, through voices that know the culture. Uncle Ramesh in Queens sees things one way. Brother Winston in Georgetown sees them another. Yard Report in Kingston disagrees with Cousin Leroy in the Bronx. Together they triangulate what the headlines leave out.

**The Trade** — monthly long-form essays. Concrete stories, real data, and the mechanisms behind the things these places do well. The founding essay is [*What Ghana and Guyana Have in Common Besides the First Three Letters*](/the-trade/ghana-guyana-essay/).

## Who this is for

People born in these places. People whose parents were. People whose curiosity runs in the right direction. And anyone — journalist, analyst, researcher, organizer — who needs reporting that doesn't require them to already know the context.

## How we work

We read the four Guyanese daily papers every morning — Chronicle, Kaieteur News, Guyana Times, Demerara Waves. We read across the Caribbean and across the African countries we cover. Then we write the brief.

Our satirical columns target policies and situations, not individuals. This is deliberate. The Caribbean has a long tradition of political satire that punches up at power without naming its targets — Sparrow, Merciless, Chalkdust. We work in that tradition.

Our serious reporting names individuals when the public record already has. If multiple Guyanese papers have published a story, we can report it. If the story is ours alone, we protect our sources and our staff.

## Who we are

The Tradewinds Brief is edited by **Ajay Messiah**, a writer working from the diaspora. The publication is independently owned and operated by Hatchling Media, a New York–based IP studio. We take no advertising that would compromise our independence.

If you want to know why this matters to us, read [the founding essay](/the-trade/ghana-guyana-essay/). If you want to reach us, see [Contact](/contact/).

## Our standard

The Tradewinds Brief writes for readers who already care. We don't over-explain the basics. We assume the context and do the work that follows from it.

That's the contract. Show up every morning, tell you what happened, and respect that you can take it from there.
