De Boys Seh — April 6, 2026

Speedeet and Wilar debate Easter kite regulations, whether CARIFTA medals count as homework excuses, and the ongoing philosophy of the $100,000 cash grant.
April 6, 2026 · 3 min · 442 words · Speedeet and Wilar
From The Tradewinds Brief — daily diaspora reporting. Subscribe Free →

De Boys Seh — Speedeet and Wilar from Pike Street with their Sunday take on the week.

WILAR: Speedeet. You know dey say de government announcing a digital registry for drivers?

SPEEDEET: Yeah. Track all yuh tickets and violations.

WILAR: You think they doing one for kite flyers too?

SPEEDEET: (pause) For kite flying?

WILAR: Our kite hit a woman roti today.

SPEEDEET: That was an accident.

WILAR: An accident that could be in a registry.

SPEEDEET: Wilar. There is no kite registry.

WILAR: Not yet.

SPEEDEET: You see Guyana get six medals at CARIFTA?

WILAR: Six! That’s plenty.

SPEEDEET: I trying to figure out if that counts as school news so I can bring it up in class tomorrow.

WILAR: Why you need to bring it up in class?

SPEEDEET: If I mention CARIFTA maybe Miss Benn forget to ask about de history assignment.

WILAR: (considering this) That’s a advanced strategy Speedeet.

SPEEDEET: Thank you.

WILAR: It won’t work.

SPEEDEET: I know. But I gotta try.

WILAR: My granny still waiting on de cash grant cheque.

SPEEDEET: Mine get hers. She put it in de bible.

WILAR: (again) In de bible?

SPEEDEET: She say God watching de money now.

WILAR: What chapter?

SPEEDEET: Psalms. Near de middle. She say Psalms is a good chapter for money because it’s de longest book.

WILAR: (nodding) That make sense actually.

SPEEDEET: It really don’t. But she’s my granny so I didn’t say that.

SPEEDEET: De government put 22,000 street lights.

WILAR: I know. My street still dark.

SPEEDEET: Same. But my cousin street in Eccles get one. She send photo.

WILAR: One light?

SPEEDEET: One light in front of she house. She very excited.

WILAR: That’s… one light.

SPEEDEET: In Eccles is a big deal. Before, she does use a torch to get from de gate to de door.

WILAR: Guyana has a long way to go man.

SPEEDEET: Yeah. But at least de torch industry doing good business in de meantime.

WILAR: Speedeet. You think de CARIFTA Games people eat good while dey in Grenada?

SPEEDEET: What kind of question is that?

WILAR: I serious. Athletes need to eat right. I wondering if dey had roti.

SPEEDEET: Wilar. Grenada have their own food.

WILAR: I know. I just… I couldn’t run fast if I ain’t eat roti.

SPEEDEET: You can’t run fast anyway.

WILAR: (offended) Excuse me?

SPEEDEET: I love you man but you running like de wind taking a day off.

WILAR: (after a moment) …True.

De Boys Seh publishes Sundays and Thursdays. Speedeet and Wilar are fictional. The cash grant is real. The bible strategy is between the granny and God.
