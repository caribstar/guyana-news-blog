## Who reads The Tradewinds Brief

Our readers are the kind of people publications like this are hard to find: educated, engaged, politically aware, and actively seeking news that treats Guyana, the Caribbean, and Africa as connected rather than peripheral.

They read from Georgetown, Port of Spain, Kingston, and Bridgetown. They read from Accra, Nairobi, Lagos, and Cape Town. They read from Queens, Toronto, Brixton, and Brooklyn. They read from wherever the diaspora has gone and wherever it's going.

What they have in common: they don't need things explained to them. They're looking for reporting that respects their context.

## What we offer

**Daily newsletter sponsorship** — Your brand in front of the subscriber list, in the voice of the publication. One sponsor per day. Rates by inquiry.

**The Trade essay sponsorship** — Monthly long-form features reach our most engaged readers. A single sponsor per essay, thoughtfully integrated. Premium placement.

**Country-specific sponsorship** — Advertise to a specific country's readership (Jamaica, Trinidad, Barbados, Ghana, Kenya, Nigeria, South Africa, Guyana). Relevant to businesses serving a specific diaspora community.

**Website placement** — Homepage and country page banner placements, limited inventory, no pop-ups, no programmatic ads, no tracking beyond standard delivery analytics.

**We limit sponsorship inventory deliberately. Most placements are sold in advance.**

## What we don't do

We don't run:

- Political advertising
- Gambling or betting platforms
- Crypto or speculative investment products
- Anything that misleads our readers about what it is

We reserve the right to decline any advertiser whose product or message conflicts with the publication's editorial values. We're a small operation and our independence is non-negotiable.

## Who advertises here

Our natural fits include: diaspora-serving businesses (money transfer, travel, legal services, real estate), publishers and cultural institutions, educational programs, regional tourism boards, artists and authors, and companies with genuine ties to the countries we cover.

If you're not sure whether you fit, write and ask. We'll tell you honestly.
