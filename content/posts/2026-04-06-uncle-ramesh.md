*Uncle Ramesh is a proud supporter of good governance, responsible development, and any government that is giving out money before an election. His views are entirely his own and coincidentally align with those of whoever is currently in power.*

---

Ah, good morning, good morning! Easter Sunday and the blessings are flowing freely, just like the government's commitment to the Guyanese people.

Let me start with the cash grant. $100,000. Going into your bank account. No fuss, no bother. Just good governance doing what good governance does — putting money where the people can reach it. Some of my neighbours who does complain night and day about everything the government do? This week they quiet. Very quiet. Because the $100,000 does have a way of quieting a man down remarkably fast.

Now I hear people asking about the banking infrastructure in rural areas. Uncle Ramesh has a simple answer for those people: get an account. This is 2026. Guyana has oil. The time for keeping your savings in a Milo tin under the bed is finished.

The Karasabai airstrip! One billion and fifty-three million dollars invested in Region Nine. You know what Region Nine is? It is the interior. The deep interior. The place that previous governments forgot about so thoroughly that they must have actively worked to forget it. This government remembered. The ribbon is cut. The planes can land. Congratulations to all Karasabai residents who can now fly instead of pray.

And the CARIFTA Games! Guyana — three gold medals on Day One! A CARIFTA record in the relay! Tianna Springer! Malachi Austin! Javon Roberts! Five medals before the first morning was even finished! Uncle Ramesh watched this news and felt something he can only describe as national pride mixed with a small amount of personal vindication, because he has been saying for years that Guyana's youth have talent that only needs investment. Well. The investment went in. Now look at them go.

On the matter of the Suriname river fees — Uncle Ramesh will say only this. A neighbour who charges you to use the river between your two countries is not truly behaving like a neighbour. The private sector is right to be vex. The government is right to be firm. And if the bridge talks must pause until Suriname finds its manners, then so be it. The Corentyne River was there before any of us and will be there long after.

The CJIA modernisation — biometric gates, facial recognition, sixteen airlines, two new municipal airports in Berbice and Lethem — this is what it looks like when a country takes itself seriously. In 2020 we had four airlines. Uncle Ramesh had to make two connections to reach a regional destination. Now? Direct. The people who said oil wealth would not reach the ordinary Guyanese should take a look at the departure terminal.

And to those writing letters to the newspaper about the 60th Independence anniversary having no theatrical production — Uncle Ramesh hears you. A nation's story deserves to be told on stage. This is one area where the government perhaps moved too quickly past the arts. The calendar is still open. It is not too late to commission something worthy. Sixty years is not a small number.

Happy Easter, Guyana. The medals are coming. The money is coming. The planes are landing in the interior. Things are moving. Uncle Ramesh is satisfied.

*(For now.)*
