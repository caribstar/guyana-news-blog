📅 EVENTS THIS WEEK#

EAST BANK INTER-VILLAGE FOOTBALL TOURNAMENT
Ongoing through the week at Eccles EE Ground. Day three action has been underway. Several upsets reported — underdogs showing up. Football fans in the area should check the schedule and get out to the ground.

NATIONAL SECONDARY SCHOOLS CHAMPIONSHIP
Athletics action continuing. Watch for results. Guyana’s young athletes representing well.

NSC/FITNESS EXPRESS GAPLF NOVICES/JUNIORS POWERLIFTING CHAMPIONSHIP
Results are in — Bailey and Satar copped Best Lifters honours. Congratulations to both.

WORLD MASTERS HOCKEY INDOOR WORLD CUP
Guyana’s master hockey stars are in England this week. Genuine podium contenders, according to reports. Something to follow.

BVI SPRING REGATTA & SAILING FESTIVAL
Running through March 29 in the British Virgin Islands for any sailing enthusiasts in the community.

📢 COMMUNITY NOTICES#

CASH GRANT REMINDER:
If you are an eligible Guyanese citizen 18 and over and have not yet received your cash grant, the government has indicated the rollout is phased. Public servants and government employees are receiving this week. Additional cohorts will follow. Ensure your bank account information is current with your employer/relevant authority.

BANK ACCOUNT REMINDER:
The cash grant is being disbursed digitally. If you do not have a bank account, you cannot receive the digital transfer. The government has been consistently encouraging all Guyanese to open accounts. This is a practical matter.

ELECTRIC MOTORCYCLE USERS:
The Guyana Chronicle has raised concerns about road safety related to electric motorcycles. The Bounty Board is not here to lecture — but if you’re riding one, please be aware that traffic laws apply to you the same as to everyone else. Just a gentle notice.

🏫 EDUCATION CORNER#

National Grade Six Assessment (NGSA) results are expected to be released on or before July 5, 2026, per the Education Minister. Parents of students sitting NGSA this year: mark the calendar.

Record 800 secondary students certified with job skills in 2025 — this was reported recently and worth noting. Vocational skills certification is expanding. Good development for students who want options beyond traditional academic paths.

🏥 HEALTH CORNER#

AIDS-related deaths in the Caribbean have declined by over 60 percent, according to Health Minister Dr. Frank Anthony. This is a significant regional public health achievement. Testing and treatment access have improved substantially across the region.

Region Three (West Demerara) Dialysis: The government has reported growing dialysis demand in Region Three and is addressing with additional support. If you or a family member needs dialysis services and has had access challenges, contact the relevant regional health authority.

GPHC-HERO Mission: Dozens of patients received free mobility-restoring procedures this week. The mission continues. Watch for upcoming announcements about future sessions.

🌍 REGIONAL NOTICES#

CCJ CASE MANAGEMENT CONFERENCE — March 25:
The Caribbean Court of Justice holds a Case Management Conference tomorrow on the Mohameds extradition matter. This will be widely reported. Worth following if you’re tracking this legal story.

COMMONWEALTH HEADS OF GOVERNMENT MEETING:
Scheduled November 1-4, 2026, in Antigua and Barbuda. Antigua’s PM Browne assumes the Commonwealth chairmanship. This is on the regional calendar.
