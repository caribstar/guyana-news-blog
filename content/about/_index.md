The Tradewinds Brief is a satirical news and commentary publication covering the Caribbean and Africa.

We publish daily briefs, regional dueling perspectives, weekly columns, and long-form analysis, written through the lens of dramatic irony, civic engagement, and the occasional rum-shop verdict.

## What We Cover

We cover Guyana, Jamaica, Trinidad and Tobago, Barbados, Ghana, Kenya, Nigeria, and South Africa through a mix of satire, commentary, and cultural reporting.

Our daily lineup includes the Guyana Daily Brief, Uncle Ramesh's pro-government commentary, regional dueling pairs, and rotating weekly features: Speedeet & Wilar (children's stories), Patriots Portfolio (Friday investment satire), DJ Roadblock (traffic), the Rumour Mill, Bam-Bam Sally, and more.

## Our Editorial Stance

We are not partisan. We are not sponsored. We are not affiliated with any political party, government office, or commercial interest in any country we cover.

We do practice satire. The voices on this site are characters with comedic personalities — Uncle Ramesh defends the government with the energy of a man who has never met a contradiction; Auntie Cheryl finds joy in objectively concerning developments. Their viewpoints are not editorial endorsements. The dramatic irony is the joke.

We follow established public-record reporting. When we name individuals, it is because multiple credentialed national news agencies have already named them on the same story. We do not break news. We respond to it.

## Who Writes This

The Tradewinds Brief is edited under the byline of Christopher Mathis. Our authored characters — Uncle Ramesh, Yard Report, Cousin Leroy, Trini Dispatch, Auntie Cheryl, Bajan Bugle, Miss Violet, Accra Almanac, Nairobi Dispatch, Naija Lookbook, Cape Chronicles, and the rest — are pseudonymous editorial voices, not real correspondents.
